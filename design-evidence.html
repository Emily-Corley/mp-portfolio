Design Evidence

Here I will discuss and explain the reasonings behind the choices I have made and the tools I have used to create this website.

Code Snippet Two Column

Typography

I made the decision to use the font ‘Fira Sans Condensed’ consistently throughout my website. The reason why I chose this font is because sans serif fonts are easy to read and are considered modern, minimalistic and are good to use for titles (Pack,2017). I also think that the font appears professional, and this was important because the intended audience for this website is media professionals who could potentially offer career opportunities in the media industry. The choice to only use one font throughout the website was made because having too many fonts can be distracting and be seen as unprofessional (Arhipova, 2017). I made headings and subheadings larger and bolder than main body text to establish a strong visual hierarchy (Graham, 2012). Having a clear typographic hierarchy makes text legible, easy to scan and draws in the attention of users (Arhipova,2017).

Colour Theory

At the start of the design process, I decided to create a blue colour scheme throughout my website. I chose this colour as research shows that the colour blue is perceived as aesthetically appealing and often associated with trust (Cyr et al, 2010). When I began to pull in my own images to my website it became clear that blue headings were hard to read against the photo I had chosen as my header photo. Therefore, I decided to include another colour simply for headings and use blue everywhere else. I wanted the colours to work well together, so I chose a colour which is opposite blue in the colour wheel, which is orange (Holtzschue, 2012). This is known as a complementary colour pair (Itten, 1970) and when used together they are meant to contrast and complement each other well.

Accessibility

Website accessibility ensures that websites are available for everyone of all different abilities to use and experience (Dudharejia,2020). A few ways in which websites can be made more accessible is by including alternative text, simplistic language, a good colour contrast and clear typography (Brophy and Craven, 2007). Alternative text is used within HTML to describe an image, this benefits people who are visual impaired, but it could also benefit those who may be in a situation where images on a website won’t load. In order to ensure the language used on my website was easy to read and understand, I used the website Hemmingway Editor to improve the readability of my text (See the supporting evidence section). Whilst creating my website I observed that the blue headings were hard to read against the header image I chose to use, therefore I needed to find an alternative colour to use as it is important to have a good colour contrast. This is beneficial for those who may have limited vision but also those who may be looking at the website outside or in direct sunlight. Finally, typography also plays a part in making a website accessible because it is important that any text is easily legible to users. I chose to just include one font because having to many fonts on one website can make it much harder to read and understand, which hinders the experience that users will have on the website.
